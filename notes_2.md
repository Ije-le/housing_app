# NOTES

I decided to try a dashboard (app_v2.py) instead of a search app, but the problem here was the difficulty with knowing what items fall under what category. To fix this, I created a full items list, so that users could simply browse through the list if they were looking for particular items.
For the details page, I had copilot summarize key occurrences in an item's timeline, and display that as a short note. Users interested in learning more can click on the actual timeline to see more details.

One major problem I encountered with building this app is that the minutes themsleves were not written in very conversational language or in too many words, so the summaries sounded like them. In trying to merge multiple excerpts together and produce insightful summaries, I was getting summaries that looked like this:

Board discussed fire extinguisher in March 2022. In March 2023, inspection scheduled for April 5. In April 2024, fire extinguisher inspection mentioned.

Multiple attempts to make the summaries sound and flow better did not help much, but what eventually helped was exporting the summaries in to a json file (manual_blurbs) and manually editing them. I told copilot to update the script so that the manual summaries in the json file can overwrite those generated by the script. That worked better.

I caught a few other errors while manually cleaning up the summaries and while factchecking the output with the pdfs themselves Example:
The app had an item labelled "entrance door" and the summary for that read: "In July 2021, residents liked the new Board meeting entrance door at Attick Towers."
When I checked the pdf, residents did not say they liked the door. Rather, they liked a notice placed on the door. Excerpt from the minutes: Ms. Lynn reported that residents of Attick Towers liked the new Board meeting notice posted on the entrance door.

One other thing I did was to prompt Copilot to refer to related issues and not just the keywords mentioned. I saw the effect of this under some items. Fire system is an example. The summary went from just highlighting the times fire systems were mentioned to this:

"In January 2022, the board approved payment to Cintas Fire Protection for $2,932.77. Since then: alarms have been reinstalled in the dining room and lobby; the property passed fire alarm inspection; City of College Park grant/ARPA grant was approved for replacing the fire suppression system. Most recently, in November 2025, smoke from a resident unit due to a burned pot prompted a Fire Department call."

I like the link back to the smoke from a neighbor's room.
I also added a simple visualization to show how frequently an item was mentioned compared to the total number of items recorded on the app.
